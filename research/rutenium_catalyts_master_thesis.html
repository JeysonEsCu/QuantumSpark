This project focused on the reaction mechanism to obtain the (E)-1,2-Diphenylethene using a Bidentate Ru(II)-NC Catalyst, based on the mechanism proposed by Gong, et. al. in 2020. The research started analyzing the Ru-PPh3 bond dissociation bond to verify the existence of an intermediate which was not considered in the original study. This step, referred to as "catalyst activation", suggested that this intermediate, Am, is energetically less stable than the catalyst, but still possible with the elimination of the PPh3 being an endothermic process. The intermediate Am is necessary due to the presence of a region where the ethoxy group (from the ethanol used as a solvent) attaches to the Ru atom while the chlorine atom is leaving, following a SN2 reaction. A scan calculate indicated the absence of a barrier for this process or a too small one to be accurately calculated. A SN1 reaction was also tested, however the energy of the resulting intermediate was significantly higher than the Am.
The new intermediate, B is more stable than the first two intermediates and is formed through an endothermic process. B is the first intermediate in the first cycle that gives as a first product the (Z)-1,2-diphenylethene. The intermediate B undergoes a rearrangement, forming an Ru-H bond (taking the acidic proton from the ethoxy group). This process results in the intermediate C and the byproduct ethanal with a reaction barrier of 12.1 Kcal/mol in an exothermic step. This intermediate C plays a crucial rol in the formation of the final product, participating in the subsequent cycle to yield (E)-1,2-diphenylethene. The interaction between the intermediate C and the ethylene occurs without a energy barrier, leading in the intermediate D, where the ruthenium interacts with the triple bond, forming a is more stable intermediate, after overcoming an small barrier, a Ru-C bond is formed resulting in an even more stable intermediate, E.
Two additional intermediates were proposed to clarify the reaction mechanism. To complete the first cycle, the catalyst must eliminate the (Z)-1,2-diphenylethene group and incorporate the ethoxy group. The first missing intermediate involves be the coordination between intermediate E and the ethanol, Em, occurring without an barrier and increasing slightly the system's energy. The intermediate Em facilitates the migration of the proton from the ethanol to the C=C double bond, leading the formation of the bound between the catalyst the the ethoxy group, resulting in the intermediate Em2. This new intermediate regenerates the catalyst as intermediate B releasing the intermediate product of the overall reaction, with ΔG = - 23 Kcal/mol.
The second cycle start with the interaction of the product of the first cycle and the intermediate C forming a more stable intermediate, F. The process D → E is repeated, but using the (Z)-1,2-diphenylethene instead of the ethylene to form the intermediate G. In this intermediate, the double bond is replaced by a single bond, allowing free rotate to adopt the most favorable and stable conformation. Since the, ethane contains two phenyl substituent, the most energetically favorable conformer is the one where the phenyl group are positioned furthest apart. Once this conformer is established, one hydrogen migrates from the single bond to the ruthenium, following the reverse process G → F. This results in the formation of the final suggested intermediate, Gm, which regenerates the catalyst as the intermediate C and giving as the final product the (E)-1,2-diphenylethene.
It is important to note that the intermediate C has a region where various compounds can interact with it, including the ethanol, which acts as a hydrogen donor and solvent. Calculation suggested that the reaction between the intermediate C and the ethanol is feasible, occurring spontaneously and exothermically. This side reaction was useful to estimate the yield of the reaction, as the concentration of the intermediate C is crucial, given its role in both catalytic cycles. A topological analysis and microkinetic analysis were performed in the paper.
