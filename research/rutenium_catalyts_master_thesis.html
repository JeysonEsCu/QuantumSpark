This project was focused on the reaction mechanism to obtain the (E)-1,2-Diphenylethene using a Bidentate Ru(II)-NC Catalyst from the mechanism proposed by Gong, et. al. in 2020, the research started analyzing the breaking of the Ru-PPh3 bond to verify the existence of an intermediate which was not considered in the original article. This step is named "the activation of the catalyst" and our calculation suggests that this intermediate, Am, is energetically less stable than the catalyst, but still possible. The intermediate Am is necessary due to the existence of a region where the ethoxy group is attached to the Ru atom while the chlorine atom is leaving, following a SN2 reaction. The new intermediate, B, is the first intermediate in the first cycle that will give as a first product the (Z)-1,2-diphenylethene. This new intermediate suffers a rearrangement forming the Ru-H bond, giving the intermediate C and the subproduct ethanol. This intermediate also participates in the next cycle to produce the (E)-1,2-diphenylethene. The interaction between the intermediate C and the ethylene is a process that does not have a barrier and the resulting intermediate, D, the interaction of the Ruthenium with the triple bond of the ethylene drives the migration of the H from the catalyst to the ethylene, forming the intermediate E. The final step is the formation of the (E)-1,2-diphenylethene and the regeneration of the catalyst.
