Kinetic study of the competition between CO2 and O2 binding to hemoglobin

This study was a part of a university project in the bachelor course "Research in chemistry A", started with the optimization of the O2 and CO2 and hemoglobin molecules. It was tested different level of theory to determine which could achieve convergence.
The software used was Gaussian 09 and the tested levels of theory tested included BLYP, PBE, B3LYP and M06, combined with the Pople's basis sets: 3-21G, 6-31G, 6-31G(d), 6-31G(d,p), 6-31+G(d), 6-31+G(d,p) and 6-311G.
As my first project in computational chemistry, I focused on analyzed geometry, vibrational frequencies,and energies differences, comparing the results obtained from each method.
The outcomes reported here are result of a new set of calculation, now using the article J. Phys. Chem. B 2015, 119, 4, 1275-1287 as a reference since the original results could not recovered.
The molecular geometries were optimized using the level of theory: UB3LYP/LANL2DZ for Fe and UB3LYP/6-31G* for the H, C, N and O. Since the molecule contains Fe(III), the charge and multiplicity used was (1, 2). However, the optimization of hemoglobin with Fe(II) was also performed.
