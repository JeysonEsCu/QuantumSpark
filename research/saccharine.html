Iron Saccharine

The synthesis of Iron saccharine started eliminating the dissolved oxygen from water through a argon flux in a Schlenk line in at inner atmosphere of argon.. The iron saccharine is prepared from a saccharine that contains iron and sulphur with heat and stirring.
Next step is the filtration of the compound, I tested it at room temperature and in a cold bath. These two methods produces two different crystal sizes, the one at low temperature produces crystals fast but small, while the one at room took time but produced bigger ones.
Another method that I tested was using small crystals obtained in previous tests in the collection glass as nucleation centers, this method produced bigger crystals. The cross contamination in all the tested method had to be avoided. Any piece of impurity could work as a nucleation center!
Another technique used was the recrystallization, using a mixture of solvents with low evaporation temperature, the compound was dissolved and then cooled down to room temperature, producing a better crystal structures.
After getting the crystals, I analyze the fusion point, a short range of fusion temperature suggesting that the compound was pure. Otherwise, the long range of temperature would suggest that the compound was contaminated.
Another test done was the solubility using different solvents. The solvents tested were methanol, DMSO, DMF, ethanol, Chloroform, THF, acetone and methanol.
The most time demanding time and excited part as a first year student was testing different synthetical routes testing different conditions of temperature, vacuum, and time.
