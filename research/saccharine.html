Synthesis of iron saccharin

The synthesis of iron saccharin started by eliminating dissolved oxygen from water using an argon flux in a Schlenk line in an inert atmosphere of argon. Iron sacchar was prepared from saccharin that contains iron and sulfur, using heat and stirring.
Next step is the filtration of the compound, I tested at both room temperature and in a cold bath. These two methods produced different crystal sizes:the low temperature method produced small crystals rapidly, while the room temperature method took longer but produced larger ones.
Another method I tested involved using small crystals obtained from previous experiments as nucleation centers, producing larger crystals.Throughout all experiments, cross contamination had to be avoided. Any piece of impurity could acts as a nucleation center!
Recrystallization was also performed using a mixture of solvents with low evaporation temperatures. The compound was dissolved and then cooled down to room temperature, producing a better crystal structures, leading to improved crystal structures.
After getting the crystals, I analyzed the melting point, a narrow melting range of temperature suggested high purity, while, a broader range of temperature would suggest that the compound was contaminated.
Additionally, I tested the compound's solubility using different solvents, including methanol, DMSO, DMF, ethanol, Chloroform, THF, acetone and methanol.
As a first year student, the most time-demanding time and exciting part of the process was experimenting with different synthetic routes varying temperature, vacuum conditions, and reaction time.
