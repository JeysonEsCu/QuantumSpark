About me

I am Jeyson Esquivel Curichimba, currently (2024) a PhD student at Bergische Universität Wuppertal in Germany working under the supervision of Hilke Bahmann in the implementation of Locally Range Separation (LRS) exchange correlation functionals to study properties of systems in excited states in the program package TURBOMOLE.
However, my interest in research is not limited to this topic. I studied the bachelors in chemistry at the National University of San Marcos (UNMSM) in Peru, where I had the opportunity to work as a lab assistant in the synthesis of Iron saccharine under the supervision of Christian Aliaga in my first year, funded by the Fund for Innovation, Science, and Technology (FINCyT) through project 364 PNICP PIBA 2014. In this project, I learned the basics of the synthesis of coordination compounds and the use of the Schlenk line.
During this year and the year after, I studied and got fascinated in the universe of math, starting with spacial analytical geometry, linear algebra, analysis and one of the most exciting topic, differential equations. During those two years, I also focus in understanding theses topics in deep.
The first course that I made me interested myself in Theoretical Chemistry was Electronic and Molecular Structure, where I learned the basics of quantum mechanics and its application in the study of the electronic structure of atoms and molecules and then Introduction in Quantum chemistry, that I studied simultaneously at UNMSM and in the Science department of the University of Engineering (UNI).
With my background in math and quantum chemistry, I started my first project studying Kinetic study of the competition between the binding of CO2 and O2 to hemoglobin using the software Gaussian, supervised by professor Dr. Cjuno. However, due to the complexity of the project, time and facilities limitations, I could not finished it, but I learned the basics of using the software and how to get and interpret results.
My next interest topic was Radiochemistry, where I learned about basic aspects of nuclear physics, radioactive processes, radioactive equilibrium, nuclear detectors, radiological protection, neutron activation analysis (NAA), nuclear fusion, radioisotopes production, nuclear reactors, application in chemistry and other areas, besides I took the lab course at the Peruvian Institute of Nuclear Energy (IPEN), being able to use real radioactive materials and facilities to analyze radioisotopes. After a successful results in the course I applied for an internship in the same Institute to work in the project Size effect of the MoO3 in the Tc-99m production founded by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) by the Coordinated Research Project No. F22068, titled "New Tc-99m Production Technologies and Tc-99m Generators", carried out under research contract No. 22460, "Optimization of a Mini-Plant for Automatic Production of Tc-99m Using a New Absorbent." presenting the preliminary results at the Iberoamerican congress of Chemsitry in 2018.
After a period working in industry as a chemical analyst, I got a scholarship from National Council for Scientific and Technological Development to study the master at University of São Paulo (USP) in Brazil, where I studied the conversion from 1,2-Diphenylacetylene to (E)-1,2-Diphenylethene using a bidentate Ru(II)-NC catalyst" published in the European Journal of Inorganic Chemistry under the supervision of the professor Dr. Haiduke, using Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Quantum Theory of Atoms in Molecules (QTAIM) to analyze each steps in the reaction. Studying a master degree in chemistry at USP, I had the opportunity to learn not only courses related to theoretical chemistry such as Advance Quantum Chemistry, Principles of Density Functional Theory, Methods of Molecular Quantum Mechanics or Statistical Mechanics , but also courses related to theoretical physics, mathematics, and programming using recorded lesson from MIT, Balseiro Institute.
and other sources.
After finishing my master, I started my PhD in Germany, as a complementary tasks, I have participated in the Symposium of theoretical chemistry (STC 2023 in Zürich), Workshop on Theoretical Chemistry 2024 in Bad Hofgastein, Turbomole: Today and Tomorrow (University of Oxford), hackathons in Berlin and Darmstadt. Besides, I supervised an summer intern, working on the project "Assessment of Novel Functionals for Optical Properties" funded by DAAD and Mitacs (Canada) and conducted some projects with master students in the Praktikum Computergestützte Berechnung von Molekülstrukturen lecture. During this period, I have studied some other courses such as "Mathematical Machine Learning" and "Introduction to Scientific Computing" in the Math department. Also I have been studying Quantum computing from recorded courses from IBM.
Out of the academic world, I practice slackline, rugby, and hiking, having trekked the Alps in Austria, as well as explored Brazil and several cities in Germany, Belgium, and the Netherlands. I also practice hurling and have played in two hurling tournaments, in Eindhoven and Köln.
